Gandhi Jayanti is a national festival celebrated in India to mark the occasion of the birthday of Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi,
 who is also known as the "Father of the Nation". It is celebrated on 2nd October. It is one of the three National Holidays in India.
Gandhiji was a pioneer of truth and non-violence. He started the 'Satyagraha' (non-violence) movement for the Indian freedom struggle.
He also played a very significant role in achieving independence for India from British rule. 
He proved to the world that freedom can be achieved through the path of total non-violence.
Various ceremonies and activities conducted at various parts of the country with many school children dressing up like Gandhi
All the organizations throughout the country remain closed on this day. 
Popular activities include prayer meetings, commemorative ceremonies in different cities by colleges, local government institutions and socio-political institutions
Special event is organized at Raj Ghat,
 New Delhi where Gandhiji was cremated. People do prayers, pay homage and sing Gandhiji's favorite song.
Painting and essay competitions are conducted and best awards are granted for projects in schools and the community encouraging
 a non-violent way of life as well as celebrating Gandhi's effort in the Indian independence movement.
Statues of Mahatma Gandhi throughout the country are decorated with flowers and garlands, and some people avoid drinking alcohol or eating meat on the day.
